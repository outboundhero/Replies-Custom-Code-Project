The Nurture Workflow

How we re-engage cooled-down leads, route them to the right campaigns in the right inbox, and put the whole funnel on autopilot — explained simply.

A complete, plain-English guide to the Nurture hub, the Automation tab, and every button.

What this is

Nurture is our second-chance engine. When a lead goes quiet, says “not right now,” is out of office, or finishes a sequence without replying — we don’t throw them away. After a 45-day cooldown, Nurture re-engages them with a fresh campaign in the correct inbox.

This guide walks through the entire flow: the screens, what every button does, and exactly how to switch a client “on” so leads start flowing automatically. No jargon — just what you see and what to click.

02A few words to know

Client (tag)

A cleaning client, shown by its short code — e.g. JPC, CCGW. Everything in Nurture is organised per client.

Group 1 & Group 2

Each client belongs to a group, which decides which inboxes their leads use. Shown as a small G1 / G2 tag.

Instance (workspace)

One of four Bison sending workspaces. Each group has a Business (B2B) one and a Personal (B2C) one.

Lane: B2B vs B2C

A lead with a company email goes to the Business inbox; a personal email (gmail, yahoo…) goes to the Personal inbox. Automatic.

ESP — G / O / S

The lead’s email provider: G Google, O Outlook, S SEGs (everything else). Each gets its own campaign.

Ready / Waiting / Added

Ready = can be contacted now. Waiting = still cooling down. Added = already pushed into a campaign.

Why groups & lanes matter

They guarantee a lead lands in the right workspace and the right provider campaign — so a Group-2 business lead never ends up in a Group-1 Google campaign. The system handles all of this for you; you just pick the campaigns.

03Where your leads live: the four inboxes

A nurture lead doesn’t just sit in one place — it is physically placed into the correct Bison inbox (workspace) for that client before any campaign sends. This is what makes sure every lead is contacted from the right sending account.

We send from four inboxes, not one

There are four Bison sending workspaces, paired into two groups. Every client belongs to one group, and each group has a Business (B2B) inbox and a Personal (B2C) inbox:

G1  Group 1

Business (B2B): OutboundHero
Personal (B2C): Cleaning Outbound

G2  Group 2

Business (B2B): Facility Reach
Personal (B2C): Outbound Clean

How each lead finds its inbox

It’s automatic, based on two things — the client’s group and the lead’s email type:

  • A lead with a company email → the group’s Business (B2B) inbox.
  • A lead with a personal email (gmail, yahoo, outlook.com…) → the group’s Personal (B2C) inbox.

The leads are already in place

Every Ready lead has already been created in its correct inbox, carrying its full details (company, city, and other personalisation) so your campaign templates fill in normally. From now on, new leads are placed into the right inbox automatically as they become Ready — you never sort them by hand.

Why this matters

It guarantees each lead is contacted from the inbox that actually belongs to that client and matches their domain setup — business leads from the business sender, personal leads from the personal sender. No cross-mixing between clients or workspaces, and deliverability stays clean.

How it connects to campaigns

Being “in the right inbox” is step one. The Target Campaigns map (Section 07) is step two: within that inbox, you choose which Google / Outlook / SEGs campaign the lead joins. Inbox + campaign together = the lead lands exactly where you intend.

04The Nurture hub (Overview)

This is the landing screen. It’s your bird’s-eye view across all clients.

The three tiles at the top

  • Ready to nurture — leads that are eligible & safe to contact right now, across all clients.
  • Waiting — leads still inside their 45-day cooldown.
  • Added — leads already pushed into a nurture campaign.

Client cards

Below the tiles, every client appears as a card with their counts. Click any card to open that client’s page. Use the Search box to find a client and the Sort menu to order by Ready, Waiting, Added or name.

The two tabs

At the top you’ll see Overview and Automation. Overview is this client-card view. Automation is the control room — next section.

This is the heart of the new workflow — your single screen to see, at a glance, which clients are ready to send and which still need setup.

The client list (reading a row)

Each row is one client. From left to right:

  • Checkbox — select clients for a bulk action.
  • Client tag + G1/G2 group.
  • The campaign matrix — for each lane (B2B and B2C) you see three provider chips: G O S. Their colour tells you the campaign’s state (see the colour key below).
  • ⚠ No map / ✓ Mapped — whether you’ve confirmed this client’s target campaigns.
  • ✓ Ready / ⚠ 2 gaps — whether all campaigns exist, or how many are missing.
  • ⚡ Auto / Off — the autopilot switch (only turns on once mapped).
  • › — the whole row is clickable; it opens the client’s page.

The controls

  • Search — jump to a client tag.
  • Refresh — re-pull the latest campaign data.
  • Select all + the bulk bar — tick several clients, then Enable or Disable autopilot for all of them at once.
  • Rows that need attention (no map, or missing campaigns) automatically float to the top.
The fastest way to see what’s left

Open the Automation tab and click the “Need mapping” tile. The list filters to exactly the clients that still need you to choose their campaigns. Work down the list; each one drops off as you confirm it.

07Target Campaigns & “Confirm & enable sending”

This is the one piece of setup you do per client. You’re telling the system: “for this client, send each kind of lead into this exact campaign.”

What you’ll see

The panel shows the client’s two lanes — Business (B2B) and Personal (B2C) — each with three drop-downs: Outlook, Google, SEGs. Each drop-down lists the matching nurture campaigns in that workspace. You simply pick the right one for each.

The two buttons

ButtonWhat it does
Save draftSaves your selections without turning anything on. Sending stays disabled. Use it if you’re mid-setup.
Confirm & enable sendingLocks in your choices and unlocks sending for this client. The badge flips to ✓ Confirmed.
Confirming does NOT send any leads

It only records your campaign choices and permits sending. No lead moves at that moment. Leads are sent when you click “Route all ready”, or automatically by the autopilot (see Section 10). Think of Confirm as flipping the safety off — you still pull the trigger separately, or let autopilot do it.

Why sending is gated

Nothing can be sent for a client until you’ve confirmed its campaigns. This is the guardrail that guarantees leads only ever go where you decided — never auto-picked.

08Every button, explained

The action buttons sit above the lead list on a client’s page.

ButtonWhat it doesWhen to use
Route all ready The main one. Sends every ready lead for this client into the campaigns you mapped, in the correct workspace — all of them, not just what’s on screen. Click again to stop. Your go-to for sending a client’s whole ready pool in one click.
Auto-route Sends only the leads you’ve selected (ticked) in the table, split by provider. When you want to push a specific hand-picked subset. Prefer “Route all ready” for the full set.
Add to nurture Sends the selected leads to one campaign you choose from the drop-down. Manual override for a one-off push to a single campaign.
Skip Marks selected leads as skipped so they leave the queue. To remove leads you don’t want to nurture.
Rollback to Ready Moves “Added” leads back to “Ready” (only shows on the Added view). Does not remove them from the campaign in Bison. To undo a push within the tool.
⚡ Auto / Off The autopilot switch (on the Automation tab and client page). Turns hands-off sending on or off. Only switches on once the map is confirmed. Leave ON to let the funnel run itself.
Rule of thumb

“Route all ready” is what you’ll use 95% of the time — it respects your mapping and sends everything correctly. The other buttons are for selective, manual control.

09How to start — step by step

To switch a client on from scratch:

1

See what needs setup

Open Nurture → Automation and click the “Need mapping” tile. You now have your exact to-do list of clients.

2

Open a client & choose campaigns

Click a client to open its page. In Target Campaigns, pick the right campaign for each lane × provider (Business and Personal → Outlook, Google, SEGs).

3

Confirm

Click Confirm & enable sending. The client’s badge flips to ✓ Mapped and sending is now unlocked. (Still no leads sent yet.)

4

Send the ready leads

Click Route all ready. The system sends the client’s entire ready pool into your chosen campaigns, in the right workspace. Watch the live progress; it stops automatically when done.

5

Make sure the campaigns are live

In Bison, confirm the mapped campaigns are Active (not Draft). Leads will sit in a draft campaign but won’t actually send until it’s activated.

6

Leave autopilot on

Keep the client’s switch on ⚡ Auto. From now on, new leads that become Ready are routed automatically — you don’t need to touch it again.

Doing many clients?

On the Automation tab you can tick several clients and use the bulk Enable/Disable bar to flip autopilot for all of them at once. (You still confirm each client’s campaigns on its own page — that’s the deliberate, per-client decision.)

10Putting it on autopilot

Once a client is mapped and its switch is ON, the system runs the funnel for you:

  • Every couple of hours, it checks each switched-on, mapped client for newly-ready leads.
  • It routes them into your mapped campaigns, in the correct workspace — exactly like “Route all ready”, but automatic and in steady batches.
  • It only ever touches eligible, safe, provider-confirmed leads — anything uncertain is held back so it can’t be mis-sent.
  • Churned clients are skipped entirely.

So the practical model is: set each client up once (map + confirm), make sure campaigns are active, and the leads keep flowing on their own. You only revisit a client to change its campaigns or pause it.

12Good to know / FAQ

“Auto” shows ON but it says “No map” — is it sending?

No. A client can be switched on by default, but nothing sends until its campaigns are confirmed. The display now reflects this: “Auto” only shows as truly ON once mapped. Until then it’s effectively off.

What counts as a “Ready” lead?

One that has finished its 45-day cooldown, passed the safety check (not a bounce, unsubscribe, or “do not contact”), and has a confirmed email provider. Anything missing one of these stays out of the send.

The client page shows “first 10,000 of 48,000” — why?

The on-screen list caps rows for speed; the tile shows the true total. “Route all ready” still processes every lead, not just the visible ones.

Leads are routed but nothing is sending.

The campaign is probably still Draft in Bison. Activate it and sending begins.

Do leads keep their personalisation?

Yes — each lead carries its details (city, company, etc.) into the correct workspace, so your campaign templates personalise normally.

Are churned clients shown?

No. Clients marked churned are hidden from Nurture automatically and never receive nurture sends.

The whole thing in one line

Open Automation → click “Need mapping” → for each client, pick its campaigns and Confirm → click “Route all ready” → make sure campaigns are Active → leave autopilot on. Done.
